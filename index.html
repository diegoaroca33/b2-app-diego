01Personal Identity›

The person who has shaped my identity most profoundly is my youngest son, Guillermo. He is twelve, dark-haired, with an enormous smile and a strength that surprises everyone REL who meets him. He has Down syndrome and has been through seven heart operations. And yet he skis, he reads, he rides a bike. From my own experience, the people REL who change us most are not always the ones we expect.

Another important aspect is the way society defines identity through appearance. People are often judged PASS by how they look before anyone asks who they actually are. I am convinced that what truly defines us is not our complexion or our CV, but how we respond when life does not go as planned.

I used to UT be more rigid, more certain. I have become PP more patient, more curious, and more willing to say: I do not know. That, perhaps, is the most honest thing I can tell you about who I am.

To sum up, this is a topic I care deeply about. I am convinced that the choices we make every day shape who we are and the world we live in. Thank you for listening.

Estructuras B2.2 en este texto

REL Relative clause "...the people who change us most are not always the ones we expect."
→ "who" introduces a clause that defines "the people". No comma = defining clause.
PASS Passive voice "People are often judged by how they look."
→ be + past participle. Subject receives the action. Formal and impersonal.
UT Used to + infinitive "I used to be more rigid."
→ Past habit or state that no longer exists. NOT: I used to being.
PP Present Perfect "I have become more patient."
→ Change from past to now, relevant in the present.

Estructura B2.2 clave: 2ª Condicional

2C 2nd Conditional — hipotético presente/futuro If + past simple → would + infinitive
"If house prices were more affordable, more young people would be able to live independently."
→ Situación irreal ahora. Importante: "were" (no "was") para todos los sujetos en registro formal.
PASS Passive — forced to, shared "Many young people are forced to rent." / "Chores are shared."
→ Enfatiza el resultado, no quién lo hace.

Estructura B2.2 clave: Not only...but also

Estructura B2.2 clave: Wish

WISH Wish + past simple — situación actual que quieres cambiar I wish + subject + past simple
"I wish I had more time for music."
→ No tengo ese tiempo ahora. Wish + past simple = lamento sobre el presente.
Wish + past perfect = lamento sobre el pasado: "I wish I had started earlier."
PP Present Perfect "I have found that writing songs is one of the most effective ways..."
→ Experience accumulated up to now, relevant to the present moment.
05Travel and Holidays›

I shop at local markets, eat where the locals eat, and try to avoid the standard itinerary. I believe that the best things you find while travelling are never listed in any guidebook.

Every trip leaves a mark that no photograph can fully capture — and that mark stays with you long after the luggage has been unpacked. Thank you for listening.

Estructura B2.2 clave: 3ª Condicional Invertida

3CI 3rd Conditional Inverted — la más sofisticada Had + subject + past participle → would have + pp
"Had I not travelled to Italy, I would never have understood..."
→ Equivale a: "If I had not travelled..." pero sin "if".
→ El "if" se omite y se INVIERTE el orden: "Had I..." / "Were I..." / "Should I..."
→ Muy formal. El examinador lo reconocerá inmediatamente.

07Health and Physical Care›

I try to walk or cycle every day, eat fresh ingredients, and get enough sleep. When I finally saw a physiotherapist, the recovery took three times longer than it would have needed. Prevention is always cheaper than cure.

Nowadays, many people overlook mental health until it becomes a crisis. Stress and anxiety do not announce themselves loudly — they accumulate. From my own experience, music, physical movement, and talking honestly with people I trust are the most effective tools I have found.

Another important aspect is the healthcare system. I am deeply grateful for public medicine in Spain. My son Guillermo has had PP seven heart operations, all within the public system. The surgeons, nurses, and specialists who have treated him REL are extraordinary people doing extraordinary work.

Mental health is still not given enough attention PASS in our society — and yet it is widely acknowledged PASS that psychological well-being is just as important as physical health. Taking care of ourselves is also taking care of the people who need us. Thank you for listening.

Estructura B2.2 clave: Pasiva

PASS Passive — para dar peso formal a las ideas be + past participle
"Mental health is still not given enough attention."
"It is widely acknowledged that..."
→ La pasiva sin agente ("by...") es más formal y sirve para generalizar.
Muy útil en ensayos y speaking formal.

09Shopping and Commercial Activities›

Another important aspect is sustainability. Fast fashion and excessive consumption damage the planet in ways that are no longer abstract — they are measurable.

If we all chose quality over quantity, 2C not only would we save money in the long run, NOBA but we would also reduce our environmental footprint significantly. Every purchase is a small vote for the kind of world we want to live in. Thank you for listening.

Estructuras combinadas: 2C + NOBA

2C + NOBA combinados "If we all chose quality, not only would we save money, but we would also reduce..."

Estructuras B2.2 en este texto

Unless we act urgently and collectively to reduce our carbon footprint, UNL the consequences of climate change will become increasingly difficult — and eventually impossible — to reverse. I hope my children inherit a world that is still worth protecting. Thank you for listening.

Intro: From my point of view, cooking at home has more advantages, particularly in terms of health, cost and cultural identity.

Body 1: To begin with, home cooking allows you to control exactly what you eat — the ingredients, the quantities, the method. For example, when I make a paella, I choose the rice, the sofrito, the stock — nothing is hidden. This means that you eat more consciously and, usually, more healthily. In contrast, restaurant food often contains more salt, sugar and fat than we realise.

Body 2: Secondly, cooking at home is significantly cheaper. In addition, it can be a social activity in itself — cooking together with family or friends is, from my own experience, one of the most genuine forms of connection. If we all cooked at home more often, 2C we would be healthier and more connected as families.

Concession: However, I also recognise that eating out offers experiences that home cooking cannot replicate PASS: discovering new cuisines, trying dishes from other cultures. Had I never eaten 3CI in a Lebanese restaurant, I would never have discovered one of my favourite foods.

Conclusion: In conclusion, although restaurants offer discovery and pleasure, I firmly believe that home cooking provides a healthier, more affordable and more meaningful experience. For this reason, I would recommend that anyone who wants to improve their relationship with food starts in their own kitchen — with a good sofrito.

4 tipos de tarea · 60 min · 20 pts
La clave es la TÉCNICA. Puedes sacar 8/10 en Reading sin vocabulario extra si sigues el proceso exacto.
Tarea 1 True / False — 8 frases · 8 pts+

Qué es: 8 frases sobre un texto. Decides TRUE o FALSE según el texto. No uses tu conocimiento general.

Regla de oro: La respuesta SIEMPRE está en el texto. Si no encuentras la frase correspondiente, busca más.

El proceso (3 pasos):

  • Lee la frase de la tarea y subraya la idea clave
  • Encuentra el párrafo equivalente en el texto
  • Compara PALABRA POR PALABRA — cualquier diferencia de significado = FALSE
Las 5 trampas más frecuentes:
1. Cuantificador cambiado: "some studies" → dicen "all studies"
2. Negación añadida: "is easy" → dicen "is not difficult"
3. Causa/efecto invertidos: A causa B → dicen B causa A
4. Demasiado extremo: texto dice "can help" → tarea dice "always helps"
5. Persona equivocada: lo dijo A → dicen que lo dijo B
Texto: "Some studies suggest mindfulness can be as effective as painkillers."
Tarea: "Mindfulness has been proven more effective than painkillers." → FALSE
(some ≠ proven; as effective ≠ more effective)
Texto: "Basil walked past with his face concealed by a black bag on his shoulder."
Tarea: "Basil was recorded with his face covered under a black cap." → FALSE
(bag ≠ cap — trampa clásica)

Tiempo: 15 min. Nunca dejes en blanco — 50% de probabilidad al azar.

Tarea 2 Párrafos → Títulos · 7 pts+

Qué es: 7 párrafos numerados. Los emparejas con 9 títulos. 2 sobran.

El título = IDEA PRINCIPAL del párrafo, no un detalle secundario.

El proceso:

  • Lee SOLO la primera y última frase de cada párrafo
  • Formula en tu cabeza la idea central en 2-3 palabras
  • Busca el título que expresa ESA IDEA (estará parafraseado)
  • Los distractores mencionan algo del párrafo pero no el tema principal
El texto puede decir "money" pero el título correcto dice "financial pressure". Busca el CONCEPTO, no la palabra exacta.
Párrafo: Una familia empieza a visitar a vecinos ancianos, van al parque, luego de compras...
Título correcto: "Gradually coming closer" ✓
Título distractor: "Meeting a neighbour" ✗ (solo describe el inicio)

Tiempo: 15 min.

Tarea 3 Insertar frases en huecos · 7 pts+

Qué es: Texto con 7 huecos. Eliges qué frase (A-I) va en cada hueco. 2 sobran.

Las frases se insertan por COHESIÓN — la frase conecta lo que viene antes y lo que viene después.

El proceso:

  • Lee la frase ANTES del hueco → anota la última idea
  • Lee la frase DESPUÉS del hueco → anota de qué empieza
  • La frase correcta conecta ambas ideas

Señales de cohesión que buscar en las frases opcionales:

  • Pronombres: "it", "they", "this" → ¿a qué se refieren en el párrafo anterior?
  • Conectores: "however", "therefore", "instead", "as a result"
  • Palabras clave repetidas del contexto cercano
Antes del hueco: "...American teachers focused on correct procedures—"
Frase D: "primarily ignoring errors and complimenting students on correct answers only"
Después del hueco: "Japanese teachers, on the other hand, rarely praised..."
→ "on the other hand" confirma que D es el CONTRASTE con lo que viene después. ✓

Tiempo: 15 min.

Tarea 4 Elegir palabra (a/b/c) · 5 pts+

Qué es: Texto con 7-10 huecos. 3 opciones de palabra para cada uno.

Proceso: GRAMÁTICA → SIGNIFICADO → COLOCACIÓN
  • ¿Qué categoría gramatical necesita el hueco? (nombre/verbo/adj/prep)
  • ¿Cuál tiene el significado correcto en este contexto?
  • ¿Cuál coloca bien con las palabras de alrededor?
Las 3 opciones suelen ser sinónimos cercanos. La diferencia está en la colocación o el registro.
Ej: feel/bear/sense + "like" → solo "feel like" existe como expresión fija.
"you don't _____ like you belong anywhere"
a) bear   b) feel   c) sense → b) feel ("feel like" = tener la sensación de)
"a _____ solution, such as staying with family"
a) casual   b) short   c) temporary → c) temporary (colocación fija: temporary solution)

Tiempo: 10 min. Gestión total: 60 min = T1:15 + T2:15 + T3:15 + T4:10 + revisión:5

3-4 tareas · 40 min · 20 pts
Cada audio se escucha DOS veces. Tienes 2 min para leer ANTES de escuchar. Úsalos siempre — es tu arma más importante.
Reglas de oro
  • LEE las preguntas ANTES de escuchar. Sin excepción.
  • El audio parafrasea — nunca usa las palabras exactas de las opciones.
  • 1ª escucha: marca tu mejor respuesta. 2ª: confirma o cambia.
  • Las preguntas van en orden cronológico en el audio.
  • Nunca dejes en blanco.
Tarea 1 Rellenar huecos — palabra exacta+

Qué es: Escuchas a alguien hablar. Completas frases con UNA o DOS palabras exactas del audio.

La respuesta es la palabra LITERAL que dicen. No parafrasees.

Antes de escuchar:

  • Lee todas las frases incompletas
  • Para cada hueco decide: ¿nombre? ¿verbo? ¿número? ¿adjetivo?
  • Predice qué tipo de palabra falta

Durante la escucha:

  • Escucha la frase completa antes de escribir
  • Escribe exactamente lo que escuchas
  • Si no captas en 1ª escucha, déjalo y vuelves en la 2ª
Frase: "sleeping with head under sheets may lead to _____ or Alzheimer's"
Predices: nombre de condición médica
Escuchas: "dementia" → escribes: DEMENTIA ✓
Frase: "A good piece of advice is to get some 7-8 hours of _____ continuous sleep."
→ Predices: adjetivo de calidad
→ Escuchas: "quality" → QUALITY ✓
Tarea 2 Emparejar extractos con títulos+

Qué es: 8 extractos de audio cortos. Los emparejas con títulos A-J. 2 sobran.

Los títulos parafrasean — busca la IDEA, no la palabra exacta.
  • Lee TODOS los títulos antes de escuchar y subraya la palabra clave de cada uno
  • Al terminar cada extracto, marca inmediatamente tu mejor opción
  • Si dudas entre dos, apunta ambas — decides en la 2ª escucha

Distractor: C — "An unconventional job" ✗ (no es el tema principal)
Tarea 3 True / False — del audio+

Qué es: 10 frases sobre lo que escuchas. TRUE o FALSE.

Igual que el T/F de Reading — pero de lo escuchado, no de lo leído. Mismas trampas.
  • Lee las 10 frases antes de escuchar
  • Durante la escucha: toma micro-notas al margen (✓ / ✗)
  • Las frases van en ORDEN en el audio — no saltan
Trampas reales del examen:
"3 years in the making" → tarea: "over 3 years" → FALSE
"face concealed by a black bag" → tarea: "face covered under a black cap" → FALSE (bag ≠ cap)
"nine million has still not been recovered" → tarea: "over nine million has been recovered" → FALSE
Tarea 4 Múltiple choice (a/b/c)+

Qué es: 6-7 preguntas sobre un audio largo. Eliges a, b o c.

Las opciones incorrectas usan PALABRAS del audio en el contexto equivocado.
  • Lee las preguntas y opciones ANTES de escuchar
  • Subraya la palabra clave de cada pregunta
  • No elijas algo solo porque escuchas esa palabra — fíjate en el CONTEXTO
Pregunta: "According to the speaker, who holds the power?"
Audio: "the power is what we wear every day. It's our clothing."
a) Citizens ✓   b) Industrialists ✗   c) The government ✗
→ "What we wear" = the consumer = citizens. El audio menciona industrialists y government en otro contexto.
Pregunta: "Clothing is one of the most _____ industries."
a) harmful ✓   b) influential ✗   c) profitable ✗
Audio: "the apparel industry has actually become the second most polluting industry in the world"
→ polluting ≈ harmful. El audio NO dice "influential" aunque sí que tiene influencia.
